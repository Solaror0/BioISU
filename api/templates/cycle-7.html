Penguin Mating Facts
A Time and Place
Breeding season is around December-April, the Antarctic Winter. During this time penguins can be seen up to 56 miles inland.1

Worthy of an emperor
Semi-loyal partners
Penguins are serially monogamous, taking 1 partner per year. 2
Food Issues
The male takes care of the chick while the female leaves go hunt for food, which can take around 2 months. In this time, the male can provide for the chick if needed with a special milk. Since the start of the breeding season, the male will have gone without food for around 110 days.1,2
